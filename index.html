About Me

I am a doctoral research fellow at Electrical and Engineering Department, Thapar Institute of Engineering and Technology, India under Professor Smarajit Ghosh's supervision. I have a thorough understanding of various estimation techniques for model-based and model-less BMS parameters like State of Charge, State of Health, Remaining Useful Life, et. cetera.

My research work focuses on developing Battery Management Systems (BMS) Algorithms with Lithium-ion batteries using Machine Learning, focusing on the Indian traffic scenario. I have developed a city-specific transient driving cycle to model the traffic pattern of Patiala, India, accurately. The methodology applies to any other city or geographic area.

I am skilled in using various supervised and unsupervised Machine Learning and Deep Learning models. I am deeply interested in Online Machine Learning deployed in scenarios where data arrives as a stream. Currently I am venturing in the world of Reinforcement Learning and its various applications.
