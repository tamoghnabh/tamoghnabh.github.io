About Me

I am a doctoral fellow at Electrical and Engineering Department, Thapar Institute of Engineering and Technology, India under the supervision of Professor Smarajit Ghosh. I am currently working on various estimation techniques for model-based and model-less battery management systems (BMS) parameters like state of charge, state of health, remaining useful life, to name a few.

I use machine learning models for parameter estimation for BMS algorithm. In my work, I am focusing on the traffic patterns and scenarios of Indian cities. I have developed a city-specific transient driving cycle to model the traffic pattern of Patiala, India, accurately. The methodology applies to any other city or geographic area.

I apply various supervised and unsupervised Machine Learning and Deep Learning models to different clustering, regression and classification problems. I am deeply interested in Online Machine Learning deployed in scenarios where data arrives as a stream. Currently I am venturing in the world of Reinforcement Learning and its various applications in BMS.
